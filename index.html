Info about Louis Riel

  • October 22, 1844: Louis David Riel is born in Rupert's Land, a Red River Settlement owned by the Hudsons's Bay Company near what is now known and Winnipeg, Manitoba.
  • 1858: Riel attends the Petit Séminaire of the Collège de Montréal as training for the Catholic priestood.
  • 1865: Withdraws fromt the priesthood in the wake of his father's death the previous year.
  • 1868: Eventually returns to the Red River Settlement to find that racial tensions had become high due to a high number of Anglophone settlers arriving in the area."
  • 1869 - Red River Rebellion: Riel becomes secretary of the newly-formed "Metis National Committee" and on November 2nd leads a group of Metis people to seize Fort Garry. On December 27th, Loius Riel becomes the president of the MNC's provisional government.
  • 1870: Riel advocates for the rights of the Metis Nation and attempts to negotiate with the government of Canada. Following a rebellion of a group representing anglophone Canadians, one member of the Canadian group, Thomas Scott, was tried for treaseon agains the provisional Metis government and was ultimately sentenced to death by a firing squad. Riel's support for this led to a military expedition to the Red River, with the aim of killing Riel. This effectively ends the Red River Rebellion. Riel flees to Montreal.
  • 1873: Riel runns unopposed in a provincial election, and a warrant is issue for his arrest. Riel flees for Montreal but remains a Member of Parialment.
  • 1874: Riel is re-elected in 1874 and fulfills one of his obligations as an MP, to sign a register at least once, while in disguise. Riel gains substantial support in Quebec.
  • 1875: In exchange for amnesty, Riel is exiled from Canada for 5 years. During this time he resides primarily in Plattsburgh, New York.
  • 1871: Marries Marguerite Monet dit Bellehumeur
  • 1882: Loius Riel is active in politics in Montana and campaigns on behalf of the Republican Party
  • 1883: Riel becomes a naturalized Citzen of tge United States.
  • 1884: Riel is persuaded to return to Canada, to advocate for the Metis people.
  • 1885: The NorthWest Rebellion: Riel experiences a worsening state of his mential health, believing himself to be a divinely selected political and religious leader. Support for Riel begins to wane.
  • In response to an increased presence of the North-West Mounted Police and rumours of troops approaching the Red River, Riel and his followers engage in open rebellion against the Canadian government including hostag taking and cutting telegraph lines. Riel is appointed the spiritual and political leader of the Provisional Government of Saskatchewan. Battle of Baloche [more info here]
  • May 15, 1885: Riel surrenders to Canadian Forces
  • July 1885: Louis Riel is tried for treason, by a jury of six English and Scottish Protestants. While the Jury finds him guilty, they reccomend mercy. Riel's execution is ordered by the judge and Sir John A. Macdonald upholds his sentence.
  • 1886: Riel's remains are laid to rest at the St. Boniface Cathedral in Winnipeg, Manitoba.
