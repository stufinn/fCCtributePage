Louis Riel

Métis leader, politician, and founder of the Province of Manitoba, Canada

About Louis Riel

  • October 22, 1844 - Louis David Riel is born in Rupert's Land, a Red River Settlement owned by the Hudsons's Bay Company near what is now known and Winnipeg, Manitoba.
  • 1858 - Louis attends the Petit Séminaire of the Collège de Montréal as part of his training for the Catholic priestood.
  • 1868 - After withdrawing from the priesthood in the wake if his father's death, Louis returns to the Red River Settlement to find that racial tensions have become high between the Métis people and Anglophone settlers arriving in the area."
  • 1869 - Red River Rebellion: Riel becomes secretary of the newly-formed "Métis National Committee" and on November 2nd leads a group to seize Fort Garry. On December 27th, Loius Riel becomes the president of the MNC's provisional government.
  • 1870 - Riel advocates for the rights of the Métis Nation and attempts to negotiate with the government of Canada. Following a rebellion of a group representing anglophone Canadians, a member of the Canadian group, Thomas Scott, is tried for treaseon against the provisional government and was is sentenced to death by a firing squad.
  • 1873 - While Louis runs unopposed in a provincial election, a warrant is issue for his arrest. He flees for Montreal but remains a Member of Parialment.
  • 1874 - Riel is re-elected in 1874 and fulfills one of his obligations as an MP, to sign a register at least once, while in disguise.
  • 1875 - In exchange for amnesty, Riel is exiled from Canada for 5 years, to the United States.
  • 1882 - Louis is active in politics in Montana and campaigns on behalf of the Republican Party
  • 1883 - Riel becomes a naturalized Citzen of the United States.
  • 1884 - Louis is persuaded to return to Canada, to advocate for the Métis people.
  • 1885 - The NorthWest Rebellion: In response to an increased presence of the North-West Mounted Police and rumours of troops approaching the Red River, Riel and his followers engage in open rebellion against the Canadian government including hostag taking and cutting telegraph lines. Riel is appointed the spiritual and political leader of the Provisional Government of Saskatchewan.
  • May 15, 1885 - Riel surrenders to Canadian Forces during the Battle of Baloche
  • July 1885 - Louis Riel is tried for treason, by a jury of six English and Scottish Protestants. While the Jury finds him guilty, they reccomend mercy. Riel's execution is ordered by the judge and Sir John A. Macdonald, the Prime Minister of Canada, upholds his sentence.
  • November 16, 1885 - Louis Riel is hanged for treason.
  • 1886 - Riel's remains are laid to rest at the St. Boniface Cathedral in Winnipeg, Manitoba.
